X-Ray Formation

Bremsstrahlung and Characteristic X-Rays

This lesson will focus on X-Ray formation at the atomic level, describing how X-Rays are produced through the 2 following processes: Bremsstrahlung and Chracteristic Radiation. We learn how to calculate the wavelength of produced X-Rays, and analyse the continous radiation wavelength spectrum produced by X-Rays.

Introduction

The production of X-Rays involves three steps. Firstly, there must be a source of free electrons. Secondly, those electrons are accelerated from rest. This is typically accomplished through the use of a large potential difference in a circuit. Thirdly, those electrons must be decelerated through interactions at the atomic level, and in the process release the energy lost in the form of X-Ray radiation.

Bremsstrahlung

Bremsstrahlung is a german word composing bremsen and strahlung, meaning "to decelerate/brake" and "radiation" respectively. As the name would suggest, bremsstrahlung a process which involves electrons braking, or decelerating.

Characteristic X-Rays

Characteristic X-Rays involve the accelerated electrons colliding with an electron in the inner valence shell of the atoms, causing the outer electrons to drop shells in order to fill the vacancy, in the process losing energy. This lost energy is emitted in the form of characteristic radiation.
